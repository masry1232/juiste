Common Questions

OK, what’s investing exactly?

Investing is putting your money into financial assets such as stocks, real estate, or businesses with the goal of growing your wealth over time.

Instead of just saving, which involves keeping your money safe and earning small, steady returns with interest, investing offers you the possibility of earning higher returns that usually come with a higher level of risk.



How do I buy and sell stock on Cash App?

You can buy stocks and ETFs (Exchange Traded Funds) with Cash App. First, go to the Stocks section of the app, search for a company name or ticker symbol, and then choose how much of the stock you want to buy. You can choose a preset amount or a custom amount you enter. 

Stocks can be purchased using the funds in your Cash balance. If you don't have enough funds available, the remaining amount will be debited from your linked debit card.

Once the order is completed, you can view your investments by going to the Money tab and tapping Stocks.

Stock purchases or sales can be scheduled 24/7. However, orders will only be placed during market hours. The U.S. stock market is open Monday–Friday during the following hours: Eastern time: 9:30 AM - 4 PM Central time: 8:30 AM - 3 PM Mountain time: 7:30 AM – 2 PM Pacific time: 6:30 AM – 1 PM.

If you want to sell stocks or ETFs using Cash App, go to the stocks you own on Cash App, select the company whose stock you want to sell, and then choose or enter how much of the stock you want to sell.

The amount of your sale net any applicable fees will be automatically deposited into your Cash balance. Depending on market activity, sales proceeds may take up to 1 business day to be deposited in your Cash balance.

Whether you're just getting started with trading or you already have a portfolio, Cash App makes buying, trading, and selling stock and ETFs easy.

What are fractional shares, and how do I buy them?

Fractional shares are less than one whole share of a company. With Cash App, you can buy fractional shares of a company’s stock with as little as $1.

First, search for a company name or ticker symbol in Cash App, then choose how much of the stock you want to buy, either from a preset amount or a custom amount you enter.

Fractional shares are not transferable. For a complete explanation of conditions, restrictions, and limitations associated with fractional shares, see our Customer Agreement.

Does Cash App charge a commission fee?

No, Cash App doesn’t charge commission fees when you buy or sell stocks or exchange-traded funds (ETFs).

Many brokerage accounts may charge commission fees. With Cash App, it’s free to open an account, and we don’t have minimum balance requirements or charge commission fees.

While Cash App doesn’t charge any fees per trade, there may be fees assessed that are required by government agencies, such as the SEC. We’ll disclose these fees prior to your confirmation of a trade, and they will be listed in the trade confirmation.

What are some helpful investing terms to know?

Brokerage account
A brokerage account is an account for buying and selling investments such as stocks and Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs).

Brokerage Firms
A brokerage firm is a company that facilitates the purchase and sale of securities such as stocks. Cash App’s brokerage firm is Cash App Investing LLC, a subsidiary of Block, Inc.

Commission fees
Certain brokerage firms charge a fee in exchange for their services to help you buy or sell stocks. Cash App Investing does not charge a commission fee.

Dividends
Dividends are payments made in cash or in shares by certain companies.

ETFs
Exchange-traded funds (ETFs) are bundles of multiple stocks or other investments traded as one. These bundles are selected from parts of the market such as certain industries or market indexes.

Fractional shares
Fractional shares are less than one whole share of a company. With Cash App, you can buy fractional shares of a company’s stock for as little as $1.

Investment portfolio
An investment portfolio is a collection of all your financial investments.

Stock market
The stock market is a network of exchanges where companies issue shares and other securities to investors for buying, selling, trading, and negotiating stock prices.
